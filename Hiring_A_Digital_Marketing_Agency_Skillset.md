COACHING PHILOSOPHY EMBEDDED BELOW — READ BEFORE PROCEEDING:

This skillset is rooted in the Phenom Coaching philosophy of Sanjay Wadhwa, Business Coach and Founder of Phenom Business Coaching Systems Pvt. Ltd. The philosophy is built on four foundational pillars for digital marketing readiness:

PILLAR 1 — STRATEGY AND DIRECTION: Clarity of goals, Ideal Customer Profile (ICP), and Unique Value Proposition (UVP) before any marketing begins. Vague goals produce scattered campaigns. Clarity gives momentum; confusion drains resources.

PILLAR 2 — INTERNAL SYSTEMS READINESS: Website, lead handling, CRM, follow-up process, and sales funnel must all be solid before ad spend begins. The agency can only amplify what already works. If the foundation is broken, even the best campaigns fail.

PILLAR 3 — EDUCATED CLIENT MINDSET: Business owners must focus on impact KPIs — Qualified Leads, Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Return on Ad Spend (ROAS), Revenue Growth, Conversion Rate, Customer Lifetime Value (CLV). Agencies default to vanity metrics — impressions, clicks, follower growth. The business owner's job is to demand business-driven results, not activity reports.

PILLAR 4 — AGENCY EVALUATION AND MANAGEMENT: Research and selection of the right agency partner, not just a vendor. Setting sharp KPIs. Managing the relationship proactively. Providing specific feedback. Treating the partnership as a co-piloting arrangement where the business owner holds the destination map.

FIVE SALES FUNNEL LAWS embedded in the philosophy:
1. Do not sell to cold traffic. Build trust first.
2. Create an entry-point offer (lead magnet, free resource, tester) before the core offer.
3. Capture contact details before running ads.
4. Nurture with value-adding content before selling.
5. At least 7-10 touchpoints before writing off a lead.
